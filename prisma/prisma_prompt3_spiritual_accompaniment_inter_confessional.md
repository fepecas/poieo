Search for academic and technical literature with scientific rigor on spiritual accompaniment (independent of religious denomination), with geographic priority in:

1. Colombia  
2. Latin America  
3. Western contexts (Europe and North America)  
4. Global (only if prior evidence is insufficient)  

## Operational Definition (KEY)

Spiritual accompaniment = structured practices of personal guidance oriented toward:

- meaning in life  
- decision-making  
- emotional regulation  
- purpose or vocation  
- support during difficult life processes  

Includes:

- spiritual direction  
- spiritual mentoring  
- spiritual counseling  
- structured pastoral accompaniment  

Excludes:

- purely doctrinal theology  
- philosophical reflections without intervention  
- religious texts without empirical evaluation  

---

## Search for Evidence On:

### 1. Effects of Spiritual Accompaniment

- behavior (actions, habits, discipline)  
- persistence in goals  
- decision-making  
- psychological and emotional well-being  

---

### 2. Accompaniment in Youth or Life Transitions

- education to work transition  
- vocation, purpose  
- contexts of vulnerability  

---

### 3. Mechanisms of Action

- how accompaniment operates:  
- guided reflection  
- structured dialogue  
- accountability  
- meaning-making processes  

---

### 4. Comparison with Other Interventions

- coaching  
- non-spiritual mentoring  
- psychological interventions  

---

### 5. Measurement and Impact

- how the effects of spiritual accompaniment are measured  
- indicators used  
- methodological limitations  

---

## RIGOR CRITERIA (MANDATORY)

Prioritize:

- empirical studies (quantitative or qualitative with clear methodology)  
- program evaluations  
- systematic reviews  
- university-based research  

Accept:

- qualitative studies with clear design (interviews, ethnography)  

Exclude:

- devotional texts  
- essays without method  
- exclusively theological literature  

---

## PRIORITY SOURCES

- universities (Colombia and Latin America)  
- academic journals in psychology, sociology, education  
- organizations such as:  
  - CEPAL  
  - Catholic or Jesuit universities (only if empirical research is present)  
  - research centers on religion and society  

---

## OUTPUT

1. Synthesis (not a list)  
2. Types of accompaniment identified  
3. Evidence of impact (with level of rigor)  
4. Explanatory mechanisms  
5. Gaps in Colombia and Latin America  
6. Implications for accompaniment-based interventions  

---

## FINAL FILTER

If a study:

- does not have a clear method  
- does not measure effects  
- is purely theological  

→ discard it  

---

## OBJECTIVE

Identify whether spiritual accompaniment:

- has observable effects on behavior and persistence  
- can be considered a structured intervention comparable to mentoring or coaching
