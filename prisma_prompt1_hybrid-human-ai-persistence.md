You are constructing a high-level conceptual and theoretical framework to support a research proposal focused on:

→ causal impact of hybrid human + AI interventions on **goal-directed behavioral persistence and sustained engagement** among youth (16–28) in education-to-work transitions.

## Objective

Build a structured conceptual foundation that explains:

1. WHY individuals persist or abandon goal-directed action  
2. HOW interventions (behavioral + mentoring + AI) modify this process  
3. HOW technology mediates or configures human agency  

This is NOT a general literature search. It is a **theory-building and mechanism-identification task**.

---

## REQUIRED STRUCTURE (STRICT)

Identify and organize sources into 3 layers:

---

### LAYER A — Behavioral Mechanisms (≈20%)

**Focus:**

- Behavioral science foundations of action and persistence

**Include:**

- Goal-setting theory  
- Implementation intentions  
- Self-efficacy (Bandura)  
- COM-B model  
- Present bias, procrastination, cognitive load  
- Friction, dropout, disengagement  

**Output for each source:**

- Concept or mechanism  
- How it explains persistence or dropout  
- Relevance to measurable behavioral outcomes  

---

### LAYER B — Sociotechnical Systems & Mediated Agency (≈60%)

**Focus:**

- How technology shapes, mediates, or transforms human action  

**Include:**

- Science and Technology Studies (STS)  
- Human–technology interaction  
- Distributed agency  
- Algorithmic mediation  
- Human-in-the-loop systems  

**PRIORITIZE:**

- Latin American STS if available  
- Authors like **Martin Tironi** and related scholars  

**Key questions:**

- How does technology reconfigure decision-making?  
- Does AI guide, constrain, or enable action?  
- What is the role of hybrid human–AI systems in shaping behavior?  

**Output for each source:**

- Core concept  
- Model of agency (individual vs distributed vs mediated)  
- Implications for intervention design  

---

### LAYER C — Ontological / Philosophical Foundations (≈20%)

**Focus:**

- Deep conceptualization of action, technology, and human agency  

**Include:**

- **Heidegger** (technology as revealing or enframing)  
- Philosophy of action  
- Human condition in technological systems  

**STRICT RULE:**

- Only include sources that can be directly connected to:  
  → action  
  → decision-making  
  → human–technology relation  

Avoid abstract philosophy with no operational relevance.

**Output for each source:**

- Core philosophical idea  
- Interpretation in plain language  
- Direct implication for your intervention model  

---
