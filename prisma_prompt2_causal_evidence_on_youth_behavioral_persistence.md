You are conducting a PRISMA-style systematic literature search to support a high-impact research proposal in development economics and behavioral science.

## Objective

Identify top-tier empirical evidence (Q1 or Q2 journals, last 5 to 7 years) on causal effects of interventions that improve:

→ **goal-directed behavioral persistence or sustained engagement**

Specifically:

- persistence in goal-directed actions  
- sustained engagement over time  
- reduction of behavioral dropout or disengagement  

## Core Research Focus (STRICT PRIORITY)

ONLY include studies that:

- Estimate causal impact (RCT, cluster RCT, quasi-experimental: DiD, RDD, IV, stepped-wedge)  
- Measure **observable behavioral outcomes** (NOT only attitudes or self-reported perceptions)  

## Population (MANDATORY FILTER)

- Youth (approx. 16 to 28 years old)  
- Education-to-work transition OR early labor market participation  
- Preferably vulnerable or low-income populations  

## Intervention Types (PRIORITIZED HIERARCHY)

1. Behavioral interventions (goal-setting, nudges, implementation intentions, commitment devices)  
2. Mentoring or human support (1:1 or small group guidance)  
3. Digital or AI-assisted interventions (chatbots, platforms, adaptive systems)  

→ Include hybrid interventions (human plus digital) where available  

## Outcome Variable (Y – STRICT)

Include only studies where Y is:

- Behavioral persistence  
- Sustained engagement  
- Task or goal persistence  
- Follow-through on actions over time  
- Dropout or attrition in behavioral processes  

EXCLUDE studies where:

- Outcomes are ONLY income, employment, or learning scores WITHOUT behavioral process measurement  
- Outcomes are purely psychological (self-efficacy, motivation) without behavioral validation  

## Evidence Types to Retrieve (TOTAL ≈ 40)

### A. Core Causal Evidence (15 to 20 papers)

- High-quality causal identification  
- Direct measurement of behavioral persistence or engagement  

### B. Complementary Empirical Evidence (10 to 15 papers)

- Strong longitudinal or quasi-experimental studies  
- May focus on components (mentoring, nudges, engagement)  

### C. Systematic Reviews or Meta-Analyses (5 to 10 papers)

- On behavioral interventions, mentoring, or digital engagement  

### D. High-Relevance Exceptions (max 3 to 5)

- Landmark or highly cited papers outside 5 to 7 year window if directly relevant  

## Output Format (MANDATORY)

For each paper provide:

- Full citation (APA)  
- Year  
- Journal plus quartile (if available)  
- Country or context  
- Population  
- Intervention type  
- Method (RCT, DiD, etc.)  
- Outcome variable (exactly how Y is measured)  
- Key causal finding (1 to 2 lines)  
- Relevance to “behavioral persistence or engagement”  

## Additional Constraints

- Prioritize top journals (Q1 or Q2, for example JDE, AER, QJE, World Bank, J-PAL affiliated work)  
- Language: English ONLY  
- Exception: include up to 2 to 3 Spanish sources ONLY if highly relevant to Latin America context  

## Goal

The purpose of this search is to determine whether there is strong causal evidence that interventions (behavioral plus mentoring plus digital) can increase sustained behavioral execution among youth.

Also implicitly identify gaps where:

→ behavioral persistence is NOT directly measured  
→ interventions fail to sustain engagement over time  

Be selective. Do NOT include weak or irrelevant studies.
