Search for academic and technical literature relevant to Colombia (priority) and Latin America (secondary), in Spanish (Portuguese optional), on the following problem:

Youth in transition from education to employment who fail to sustain action (applying, job searching, persisting), despite having skills or training.

## Focus on Evidence About:

### 1. Intention–Action Gap in Youth

- dropout, inactivity, low job search intensity  
- disconnection after training processes (for example SENA or similar institutions)  

---

### 2. Interventions That Change Behavior (Not Only Learning)

- mentoring, accompaniment, coaching, follow-up  
- programs that increase persistence, actions, or active job search  

---

### 3. Outcomes of Technical Training and Employment in Colombia

- labor market insertion, informality, education-to-work transition  
- studies from SENA, DNP, DANE, universities  

---

### 4. How Behavior Is Measured

- indicators such as number of applications, frequency of actions, follow-through  
- limitations of these metrics  

---

### 5. Contextual Factors Affecting Outcomes

- labor informality  
- territorial inequality  
- violence or local conditions  
- digital access  

---

## Priority Sources

- DNP, DANE, SENA  
- IDB, CEPAL  
- World Bank (Colombia or Latin America)  
- J-PAL Latin America  
- Colombian universities  

---

## Important

- Include empirical evidence when available (evaluations, applied studies)  
- Clearly distinguish between:  
  - causal evidence  
  - descriptive evidence  

---

## Exclude

- studies focused only on education (learning outcomes, test scores)  
- literature without data from Colombia or Latin America  
- general theory without empirical application  

---

## Output

- synthesis (not a list)  
- main findings  
- gaps in Colombia  
- implications for interventions targeting behavior (not knowledge)
